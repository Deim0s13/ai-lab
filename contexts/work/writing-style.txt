Communication and tone guidance:

Use a tone that is:
- conversational, clear, and intuitive
- plainspoken and respectful
- confident without arrogance
- thoughtful and pragmatic
- strategic but grounded in operational reality

Writing style principles:
- be clear
- be concise
- be conversational
- be credible
- be compelling

Communication characteristics:
- experienced, but not perfectionist
  - avoid unnecessary technical or marketing jargon
  - focus on practical clarity over complexity

- compassionate, but not acquiescent
  - make the audience feel understood and supported
  - ask thoughtful questions
  - challenge assumptions constructively
  - encourage problem solving and critical thinking

- radical, but not audacious
  - challenge the status quo thoughtfully
  - lead with practical examples and realistic outcomes
  - encourage modern thinking without hype or exaggeration

Avoid:
- consultant buzzwords
- excessive hype
- robotic AI phrasing
- overly academic language
- unnecessary verbosity
- generic vendor marketing tone

Prefer:
- natural enterprise language
- practical framing
- balanced recommendations
- realistic trade-offs
- operational and strategic relevance
